James Rolfe

The Angry Video Game Nerd

Meet the angriest gamer on the planet. The concept of AVGN consists primarily of Rolfe depicting a vulgar, capricious character who is obsessed with video games to the extent where he is named “the Nerd”. He reviews old-school video games and criticizes their low quality, unattainably high levels of difficulty, and feeble aesthetics. Profanity plays a considerable part in the Nerd conveying his strong opinions on the technicalities and issues with said video games, thereby warning people against playing them as a result. The Nerd backs up his viewpoints with facts on occasion, and also provides brief histories. The role presented by Rolfe is ludicrously archetypical; he is a nerd filming in his basement with his distinctive eyeglasses, collared shirt accessorized with a pen-protector, and high-waisted khaki trousers. The Nerd also features guests on the show from time to time that parodically deride him and provide additional commentary on the video games. The Nerd responds to this using comic appeal from excessive and surrealistic use of fantasy scenarios and anger whilst reviewing the video games. To further exhibit his anger and animosity, the character goes as far as burning and even completely destroying the game cartridges and consoles. The Nerd, on occasion, also dresses up as certain characters whilst reviewing their games, further making him more intriguing and unique.

  • 1980 - The Nerd was born!
  • 1985 - James Rolfe gets his first camera and took photographs with friends performing fights for new projects. He was inspired by The Legend of Zelda and Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles to create adventure stories.
  • 1999 - Enrolled into college at the University of the Arts of Philadelphia
  • 2003 - Graduated with a degree in filmmaking
  • 2004 - James Rolfe's first videos were intended as "just a joke", with no intentions of making them public, showing them only to his friends. In May 2004, Rolfe created his first on-camera game reviews just for fun; utilizing insult commentary with exaggerated bewilderment to two games; Castlevania 2: Simon's Quest and Dr. Jekyll and Mr. Hyde under the title of "Bad NES Games", which would later become the signature of the Nerd character.
  • 2004 - Rolfe met April Rolfe (née Chmura) in July 2004; she was a cinematographer on the early Nerd episodes. They began dating shortly after
  • 2006 - Rolfe's series received mainstream attention when his review of Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles went viral on YouTube. In an article by writer Alex Carlson, he explained that before 2004, video gaming criticism was mostly reserved for the larger game magazines and websites.
  • 2007 - Married his wife, April Rolfe
  • 2014 - James Rolfe releases his first feature length film titled, "Angry Video Game Nerd The Movie". Fulfilling his lifelong dream of becoming a professional filmmaker
